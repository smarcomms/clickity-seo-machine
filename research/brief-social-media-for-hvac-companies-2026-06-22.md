- Which platforms (Facebook + Instagram lead; Google Business Profile; YouTube/TikTok for demos)
- What to post (post ideas / content types — the most-linked angle)

**Content gaps (Clickity's opening):**
- **Outcome framing.** Everyone lists tactics; nobody ties it to **calls, booked jobs, and revenue**. This is the "turn content into revenue" wedge, perfectly suited to a trade business that counts jobs, not likes.
- **Seasonality as a strategic spine.** HVAC has hard seasonal demand (cooling season, heating season, shoulder-season slumps). No competitor builds the content strategy around the demand calendar. Clickity should.
- **The "who actually does this" problem.** Every guide assumes a busy HVAC owner will find time to post 4x/week. They won't. The honest answer — done-for-you with a human steering it — is under-served (only thin agency pages occupy it).
- **No judgment layer named.** All competitors are either software (DIY) or generic agencies. Nobody articulates AI-for-volume + human-for-strategy. That's Clickity's structural angle.

**Differentiation strategy:** Out-teach the agency pages, out-position the software guides. Genuinely useful HVAC-specific strategy (with seasonal calendar + post ideas) framed entirely around booked jobs, landing on the honest "you shouldn't be doing this at 9pm — here's the done-for-you option."
