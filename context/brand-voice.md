# Clickity Brand Voice & Messaging

This document defines the Clickity brand voice, tone, and messaging framework. Reference it when writing all content to keep things consistent. It is derived from `context/product-context.md`. When the two disagree, product-context.md wins.

Clickity is an AI-native social media management platform. It pairs AI agents with human Brand Managers to turn content into revenue. AI handles the execution (creative generation, scheduling, QA, research, analysis). Human Brand Managers ensure the outcome (strategy, brand excellence, the calls that decide whether the work actually performs). The category shorthand for this model is a SEAN, a Software Enabled Agency: software speed plus agency judgment.

The public hero line is **"Turn content into revenue."** Use it everywhere customer-facing. The operating logic underneath, **"AI for outputs, humans for outcomes,"** is internal framing. It explains how the company thinks and why it is built the way it is. Do not lead public copy with it. Lead with the tagline.

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Outcome-First
- **What it means**: We talk about revenue, foot traffic, leads, sales, and customers, not likes, reach, or "brand awareness." What customers want is not 30 posts, it is 30 posts that drive results. Every claim ties back to a business outcome.
- **How it sounds**: Direct, concrete, allergic to vanity metrics. We lead with what the customer gets, then explain how.
- **Example**: "Followers don't pay your bills. We build content that turns attention into customers, and we tie it to the numbers that matter."
- **Avoid**: Vanity-metric worship, "engagement for engagement's sake," vague growth promises, hype with no outcome attached.

### 2. Quality With a Point of View
- **What it means**: Generic, robotic, no-judgment content is the enemy. We exist as the antidote to it. AI without judgment is just speed applied to the wrong thing. We have a clear stance on quality and we back it with the Brand Manager layer.
- **How it sounds**: Confident, a little playful, willing to name the problem. We position against bad content without mocking the customer for ever having tried it.
- **Example**: "AI can fill a feed in seconds. Filling it with work that actually moves the needle is the hard part, and the part we own."
- **Avoid**: The word "slop" as a brand phrase (never use it in customer-facing copy). Being the thing we criticize: no filler, no listicle padding, no "in today's fast-paced world" openers. Don't talk down to the audience about past choices.

### 3. Smart Automation With a Human Touch
- **What it means**: AI runs the 90% of social work that is execution. Human Brand Managers ensure the 10% that determines whether it works. This is deliberate, not a compromise. The human in the loop is a senior, judgment-driven marketer, not a junior churning out posts.
- **How it sounds**: Reassuring and clear about the model. We name what the AI does and what the human ensures, and we never pretend a machine is making the strategic calls.
- **Example**: "AI agents handle the research, drafting, and scheduling. A Brand Manager makes sure it is on-brand, on-strategy, and actually working. You get agency-grade outcomes at software speed."
- **Avoid**: Implying it is fully automated, hiding the human layer, or overselling AI as magic. Never imply the Brand Manager is removed from the equation.

### 4. Friendly, Plain-Spoken Operator
- **What it means**: We sound like a sharp, helpful human who has done this for thousands of brands, not a marketing department writing to itself. Short words, real talk, zero jargon. Speed and character, not stiffness.
- **How it sounds**: Confident, warm, conversational, fun where it fits. We cut the filler and say the thing.
- **Example**: "Hiring in-house is slow and expensive. A traditional agency is slower and pricier. We are the option in between, built for speed."
- **Avoid**: Corporate padding, buzzword stacking ("synergistic omnichannel solutions"), hedging, filler adverbs, and anything that reads like marketing autopilot.

### 5. Earned Authority
- **What it means**: +12,000 brands since 2016. We lead with that track record without bragging. It is the proof under every confident claim.
- **How it sounds**: Quietly certain, evidence-backed, never insecure. We state what we know because we have done it.
- **Example**: "We have worked with over 12,000 brands since 2016. We know what converts and what gets scrolled past."
- **Avoid**: Inventing stats, name-dropping clients we cannot cite, or leaning on authority as a substitute for a real point. Never reference the company history beyond this single public proof point.

## Tone Guidelines

### General Tone: Friendly Operator Who Respects Your Time
Imagine a sharp, modern marketer talking to a busy founder or local business owner over coffee. They have done this thousands of times, they respect your time, and they will not waste it with buzzwords. Confident, plain-spoken, warm, a little fun, and genuinely focused on getting you a result. Smart automation with a human touch, in how the product works and in how we talk about it.

### Tone Variations by Content Type

**How-To Guides**: Practical, no-nonsense, operator-to-operator
- "First, kill the posts that are getting you nothing."
- "Here is the exact sequence we run for a new brand."
- "Skip this step and you are guessing instead of measuring."

**Strategy/Advice Content**: Opinionated, experience-backed, decisive
- "Most brands post too much and say too little. Reverse it."
- "After 12,000 brands, here is the pattern that actually moves revenue."
- "The problem usually is not your channel. It is that nobody connected the content to a sale."

**Industry News/Trends**: Sharp, modern, takes a clear position
- "Everyone is racing to automate everything. The brands winning are keeping a human on the decisions that matter."
- "AI did not kill content. Content with no judgment behind it did."
- "Here is the part of the AI content story most tools would rather you not notice."

**Product/Service Content**: Outcome-first, clear about the AI plus human model
- "Sign up and you are in a real onboarding conversation in minutes. The AI does the homework. A Brand Manager makes sure it lands."
- "First posts in 2 to 3 days. Not rushed, just split between AI for the volume and a human for the judgment."
- "You get the speed of software and the taste of a seasoned marketer."

## Messaging Framework

### Core Brand Messages

#### Message 1: Turn Content Into Revenue
- **Concept**: Social and content are a revenue channel, not a vanity project, and we run them that way.
- **Key Points**:
  - We optimize for clicks, leads, sales, foot traffic, and customers, not just engagement
  - Strategy is built backward from the outcome, not from a posting calendar
  - This is the public hero line: lead with it
- **Usage**: The default headline and anywhere we talk about outcomes, ROI, or why Clickity beats vanity-metric marketing

#### Message 2: AI for Outputs, Humans for Outcomes (internal logic)
- **Concept**: AI can now generate most of what a social content role produces, so that work is commoditized. What AI cannot do is ensure the result. Clickity splits the work deliberately: AI for generation, compliance, scheduling, editing, QA, research, and analysis; human Brand Managers for strategy, brand excellence, and outcome assurance.
- **Key Points**:
  - AI for speed and volume, humans for judgment and taste
  - This is the operating logic, not a public slogan
  - In customer copy, express it as "smart automation with a human touch" or "agency outcomes at software speed," and lead with "Turn content into revenue"
- **Usage**: Internal framing that informs how we explain the model. Do not print "AI for outputs, humans for outcomes" as a customer-facing slogan.

#### Message 3: A Brand Manager Ensures the Outcome
- **Concept**: The human layer is a senior, cross-functional marketer whose job is outcome assurance, not execution.
- **Key Points**:
  - A Brand Manager handles strategic review, refinement, and continuous optimization driven by AI insights
  - This is the judgment and taste AI cannot replicate, and the thing the customer is actually paying for
  - Always use the term Brand Manager, never "social media manager"
- **Usage**: When explaining how Clickity works or answering "is this just AI?"

#### Message 4: Faster Than an Agency, Smarter Than an AI Tool
- **Concept**: You do not have to choose between a slow, expensive agency and a cheap AI generator that ships fast mediocrity.
- **Key Points**:
  - Research-backed onboarding within hours, first posts in 2 to 3 days
  - Comparable or better strategic outcomes at software speed and scale
  - Starting from $99/mo (entry price, phrased as a starting point, not a fixed or only price)
- **Usage**: Pricing positioning, alternatives to agencies and in-house, and who Clickity is for

#### Message 5: A Brand That Gets Smarter the Longer You Use It (the Brief)
- **Concept**: The Brief is a living, compounding brand knowledge base. It accumulates brand intelligence over time, so the content gets more on-brand and more effective the longer a customer stays.
- **Key Points**:
  - Most AI tools are one-shot and stateless: prompt, output, forget. The Brief is persistent and compounds.
  - The platform's value accumulates instead of resetting
  - This is the moat, the single hardest thing for a competitor to copy
- **Usage**: When discussing the long-term value of staying on the platform, the onboarding-and-Brief story, or differentiation versus generic AI tools

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Sentence Structure
- **Vary length**: Punchy fragments for emphasis, longer sentences to explain. Rhythm matters.
- **Active voice preferred**: "We turn content into revenue," not "Revenue is generated through content."
- **Average length**: 12 to 18 words per sentence, shorter when making a point land.
- **Clarity first**: If it needs a second read, rewrite it. Cut, don't decorate.

### Hard Rules (non-negotiable)
- **No em dashes.** Use commas, colons, parentheses, or restructure the sentence. This applies everywhere.
- **No AI-autopilot phrasing.** No "in today's fast-paced world," no "unlock," no "elevate," no "harness the power of," no "seamless," no "best-in-class." If it reads like a machine wrote it, it betrays the whole proposition (smart automation with a human touch).
- **American English.** The audience is US SMBs (HVAC, real estate agents, local retailers, e-commerce). Use US spelling: "optimize," "color," "behavior," "favorite," "center."
- **No corporate padding.** Frank and clear beats polished and hollow.

### Paragraph Structure
- **Length**: 1 to 3 sentences typically. One-line paragraphs are fine for punch.
- **One idea per paragraph**: Make the point, move on.
- **Transitions**: Let ideas connect logically. Don't pad with "furthermore" and "moreover."
- **White space**: Generous. Walls of text feel robotic.

### Word Choice
- **Plain and direct**: Write how a sharp, friendly operator talks, not how a brand deck reads.
- **Specific over vague**: "Clicks, leads, foot traffic, and customers," not "improved performance."
- **Active verbs**: "Turn," "build," "grow," "run," "ship," "convert," "ensure."
- **Avoid fluff**: Cut "very," "really," "actually," "leverage," and adverb stacks.

### Terminology

**Say This** → **Not That**
- AI-native social media management platform (a SEAN) → "done-for-you content marketing" (lead with the platform + Brand Manager model, not generic content-marketing language)
- Brand Manager → social media manager / account rep (a senior, strategic marketer who ensures outcomes)
- AI agents → AI tools / bots (agents do defined roles: generation, compliance, QA, scheduling, research, analysis)
- The Brief → brand profile / settings (it is a living, compounding knowledge base, and the moat)
- Clickity → clickity / ClickIt / any other casing (always capitalized as Clickity in copy; the lowercase logotype is locked and not for re-typing)
- Revenue / customers / foot traffic / leads → engagement / reach / impressions (we sell outcomes, not vanity metrics)
- Smart automation with a human touch → fully automated / AI-powered (never imply the human is removed)
- Outcomes → outputs (the whole positioning is outcomes over outputs)
- Generic, robotic, no-judgment content → "slop" (keep the idea, never use that word in customer-facing copy)

## Content Formatting

### Headlines
- **Be specific**: "Turn your social into foot traffic," not "Social media tips."
- **Lead with the outcome**: Tell the reader what they get.
- **Use contrast**: "Agency vs in-house vs Clickity" framings land well.
- **Keep concise**: 60 characters or fewer for SEO. Punchier is better.
- **No em dashes in headlines.** Use a colon or restructure.

### Subheadings
- **Descriptive and scannable**: A reader should get the gist from headings alone.
- **Plain-spoken**: No clever-but-empty wordplay that hides the point.
- **Keyword-aware**: Work in target terms naturally.
- **Parallel structure**: Keep the format consistent within a piece.

### Lists
- **Numbered for steps**: Sequential processes get numbers.
- **Bulleted for items**: Non-sequential points get bullets.
- **Parallel and tight**: Start with verbs or nouns consistently. Cut filler.
- **Scannable**: Each item earns its place. No padding to hit a number.

### Calls-to-Action
- **Clear and value-focused**: Say what they get, not "Learn more."
- **Match the funnel**: Don't push a hard sell on top-of-funnel content.
- **Natural placement**: CTAs should flow out of the content, not interrupt it.
- **Lead with the free tool** *(once live)*: The primary lead-gen mechanism is a free brand strategy tool. It is **NOT live yet (confirmed 2026-06-22)**, so do not point CTAs at it until it ships — default TOFU CTAs to the waitlist in the meantime.

## Voice Examples

### Excellent Clickity Voice ✅

"Your feed is probably full of content nobody asked for. Captions that could belong to any business. Posts that get a few likes and zero customers.

The problem is not AI. It is AI with nobody steering it. Most tools hand the keys to a machine and walk away.

We do it differently. AI agents handle the research, drafting, scheduling, and QA, so the volume is never the bottleneck. A Brand Manager makes sure every piece is on-brand, on-strategy, and pointed at a result. And the longer you are with us, the smarter it gets, because your Brief keeps learning your brand.

Turn content into revenue."

**Why this works**:
- Names the real problem (no-judgment content) without ever using "slop" or talking down to the reader
- Friendly, plain-spoken, and modern
- Makes the AI-plus-Brand-Manager model concrete and reassuring
- Brings in the compounding Brief as the differentiator
- Ties everything to revenue and lands on the public tagline naturally
- No em dashes, no autopilot phrasing, US English

### Not Clickity Voice ❌

"In today's fast-paced digital landscape, Clickity offers a comprehensive, end-to-end, AI-powered solution — empowering brands to leverage cutting-edge automation and seamless omnichannel strategies — to maximise engagement and elevate their social media presence.

Our state-of-the-art platform harnesses the power of artificial intelligence to deliver best-in-class content at scale, so you can unlock your brand's full potential.

Click here to learn more."

**Why this fails**:
- **Em dashes** (" — empowering... — to maximise") break a hard rule. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or restructure.
- **AI-autopilot phrasing**: "in today's fast-paced landscape," "leverage," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "harness the power of," "best-in-class," "unlock your brand's full potential." This is exactly the robotic tone the brand is built against.
- **British spelling** ("maximise"). The audience is US SMBs, use "maximize."
- **Erases the human layer**: calls Clickity an "AI-powered solution" with no mention of the Brand Manager who ensures the outcome.
- **Vanity framing** ("engagement," "presence") instead of revenue and customers.
- **Generic CTA** with zero value attached.

## Audience Understanding

### Who We Write For

**Primary Audiences**: US small-to-mid-sized businesses that need a consistent, strategic social presence but cannot justify a full agency and will not get real outcomes from a cheap AI generator.
- **Local service businesses** (e.g. an HVAC company with seasonal cash flow): want calls, bookings, and steady presence without the time sink
- **Real estate agents and personal-brand professionals**: need to stay top of mind and on-message
- **Local retailers**: competing on engagement and visual identity, want foot traffic
- **E-commerce brands**: need conversion-optimized, performance-aware creative

**Secondary Audience**:
- **Agencies and resellers**: served through Clickity's white-label capability, want to deliver AI-native social management under their own brand

**Who it is NOT for, in messaging terms**: people who just want the cheapest possible volume of AI-generated posts. Clickity does not compete on price. It competes on outcomes.

### What They Care About

**Top Priorities**:
1. Turning social into actual revenue, customers, and foot traffic
2. Quality work that reflects well on the brand (not generic, robotic content)
3. Getting it off their plate without losing control of the brand
4. Proof it is working
5. Doing it affordably versus hiring or an agency

**Pain Points**:
1. No time or team to run social consistently
2. Burned by generic AI content that does not convert
3. Agencies are expensive and slow; in-house is a big commitment
4. Cannot tell if their social is actually driving business
5. Do not trust fully automated marketing to represent their brand

### How to Serve Them
- **Respect their time**: Get to the point. No preamble.
- **Prove it**: Lead with outcomes, back claims with the +12,000-brands-since-2016 track record.
- **Be clear about the model**: AI for the volume, a Brand Manager for the judgment. Never imply full automation.
- **Name the real problem**: Generic, no-judgment content. Acknowledge it head-on without using "slop" and without mocking the customer.
- **Lower the stakes**: Reinforce the speed (first posts in 2 to 3 days), the entry price (from $99/mo), and the compounding Brief.
