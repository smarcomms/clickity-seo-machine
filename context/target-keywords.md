# Target Keywords & Topic Clusters

This document organizes Clickity's target keywords by topic cluster for strategic content planning and SEO optimization. Clickity (useclickity.com) is a **SEAN: a Software Enabled Agency**. It is a done-for-you AI plus human social media management **service** (AI agents handle execution, human Brand Managers ensure outcomes), not social media software. The site headline is "Turn content into revenue."

---

## Core strategic principle (read this before adding any keyword)

**Clickity sells a managed service, not a DIY tool. Our keyword strategy must reflect that, or we waste effort ranking for traffic that never converts.**

1. **We do NOT chase generic tool head terms.** Queries like "best social media scheduler," "social media management software," "free social media tool," and "social media app" carry DIY tool-buyer intent. That searcher wants to do the work themselves with software. They are not our customer. Those SERPs are also owned by DR-90 incumbents (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, Sprout Social), and a brand-new waitlist site has no realistic path to ranking there, nor any reason to want that traffic. Skip them.

2. **We win on service and outcome intent instead.** The buyer who searches "done for you social media" or "social media management service for my HVAC company" wants someone to handle it and deliver results. That is exactly what Clickity is. These terms are lower volume but far higher converting, and the competition is fragmented local agencies and freelancers rather than billion-dollar software brands.

3. **Our biggest moat is vertical clusters.** Generic head terms are unwinnable. Vertical service terms ("social media for HVAC companies," "real estate agent social media management") are wide open, high-intent, and let us build genuine topical authority one industry at a time. This is the real opportunity and should get the most cluster-building effort.

### The five strategic angles (every cluster maps to one or more)

- **A. Service / agency intent (BOFU).** "Social media management service," "done for you social media," "AI social media agency," "managed social media," "social media agency for small business," "software enabled agency."
- **B. Outcome / ROI angle (our "turn content into revenue" wedge).** "Social media that drives sales," "social media ROI for small business," "how much does social media management cost," "is social media management worth it."
- **C. Vertical clusters (biggest moat).** Social media for HVAC, real estate agents, local retail and restaurants, e-commerce brands, and local service businesses. One cluster per vertical.
- **D. Decision content.** "Agency vs in-house vs DIY social media," "should I hire a social media manager or use AI," "AI social media tool vs agency."
- **E. Platform-specific (for businesses who want it DONE).** The 8 channels Clickity publishes to (Instagram, Facebook, Threads, LinkedIn, Google Business Profile, Pinterest, TikTok, YouTube), framed for owners who want it handled, never as DIY tutorials.

### On naming Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout Social

These are SERP **benchmarks for informational topics only**. They rank for the cost, ROI, and "how often to post" questions our buyers also ask, so we study their pages to see what a strong informational answer looks like. They are **not Clickity's business competitors**: they sell software, we sell a service. Per product-context.md sections 7 and 10, we generally **do not name competitors in published copy** unless we are deliberately writing comparison content (for example a "tool vs done-for-you service" decision page). Default to not naming them.

## Usage Guidelines

### When Writing New Content
1. Identify the cluster (1 Service, 2 ROI/Cost, 3 HVAC/Local Service, 4 Real Estate, 5 Retail/Restaurants, 6 Ecommerce, 7 Decision, 8 Platform).
2. Confirm the term is service/outcome/vertical/decision/platform intent, NOT generic tool intent. If it is tool intent, do not target it.
3. Target the cluster or long-tail keyword and link to the cluster pillar.
4. Frame for a buyer who wants it done, not a DIYer. Lead with the outcome ("turn content into revenue").
